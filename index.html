Our top festivals

Culture is the name for what people are interested in, their thoughts, their models.

Dashain:

Victory of Good Over Evil

Duration: 15 days

This is the longest and most important festival in Nepal, celebrating the triumph of good over evil (Goddess Durga's victory over Mahishasura) and, crucially, the strengthening of family bonds.

Diwali:

Festival of Lights Celebration

Duration: 5 days

Tihar (Deepawali) is Nepal's five-day celebration of light over darkness. It uniquely honors both deities and animals (crows, dogs, cows) for their roles in life.
Celebrate with us

Holi:

Festival of Colors

Duration: 1 days

Holi is a joyous, ancient Hindu festival that celebrates the triumph of good over evil (the legend of Holika) and the arrival of spring.
Celebrate With Us

Buddha jyanti:

Celebrating the Light of Asia

Duration: Baisakh

Observed on the full moon day of Baisakh (the 4th lunar month), this sacred festival commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and death of Gautama Buddha, who was born in Lumbini, Nepal.
Learn more

Indra Jatra:

Festival of the Living Goddess

Duration: Bhadra

It is an eight-day festival held to honor Lord Indra, the Hindu God of Rain and King of Heaven, to thank him for the monsoon and a good harvest. It also includes rituals to commemorate family members who passed away in the preceding year.

Teej

The Festival of Devotion

Duration: Bhadra

A three-day Hindu festival primarily for women, who fast and pray for the longevity and prosperity of their husbands and for a good marital life. They are famously seen dressed in red sarees.
